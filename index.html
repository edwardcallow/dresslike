Dress Like Ed Callow

For everyone else

Proceed as anticipated

This is Ed Callow

The guy who people mistake for a member of staff.
He’s usually busy doing something or other and seems
to be involved in just about everything around school.

Keep it moving

Step 1:
The jeans

A nice pair of relatively slim (but not skinny) or relaxed cut jeans. Stick to a dark rinse colour that will fit all occasions without standing out too much.

Step 2:
The shirt

Go for a (tucked in) button-up shirt with a simple, small pattern. A spread or point collar can be worn, sometimes even a button-down, though the former are much smarter.

Step 3:
The jacket

No need to over-smarten the look with a blazer or suit jacket, a sports jacket is ideal. Just be careful that the colour isn’t too similar to the jeans or it’ll look out of place.

Step 4:
The shoes

Some would say the shoes are the most important part of an outfit and Ed Callow agrees. Stick to a pair of wingtip (‘brogue’) oxfords or suede bucks to keep it casual.

Step 5:
The accessories

An outfit isn’t complete without it’s accessories. Make sure your (leather) belt matches your shoes, you’ve got bright coloured socks on and always have that staff ID card on hand.

Step 6:
The walk

It’s all well and good dressing like the man but to look like him you’ll need to perfect ‘the walk’. The key is to walk just a little faster than everyone else and look busy.

Congratulations

If you can follow these 6 easy steps you’re essentially Ed Callow.
Not so hard, is it? Well, you’ll see...
